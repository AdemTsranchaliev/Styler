History

• Manchester City are a football club in Manchester, England, founded in 1880.

• City gained their first honours by winning the Second Division in 1899; with it came promotion to the highest level in English football, the First Division.

• In the 1930s, Manchester City reached two consecutive FA Cup finals.

• During the 1934 cup run, Manchester City broke the record for the highest home attendance of any club in English football history, as 84,569 fans packed Maine Road.

The 1956 final, in which Manchester City beat Birmingham City 3–1, is one of the most famous finals of all-time, and is remembered for City goalkeeper Bert Trautmann continuing to play on after unknowingly breaking his neck.

• League Champions 1936-37, 1967-68, 2011-12, 2013-14
• European Cup Winners Cup 1970
• FA Cup Winners 1904, 1934, 1956, 1969, 2011
